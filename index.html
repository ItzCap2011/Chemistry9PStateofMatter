SOLID
  • Fixed shape & fixed volume
  • Very low compressibility
  • Particles vibrate in fixed positions
  • Lowest energy state
  • Strong intermolecular forces

  • Fixed volume
  • Low compressibility
  • Particles slide past each other
  • More energy than solids

GAS
  • No fixed shape or volume
  • Highly compressible
  • Particles move rapidly & randomly
  • High energy state
  • Fills any container

🌡️ Water State Changes
Melting & Freezing: 0°C
Boiling & Condensation: 100°C

Same temp because the process is reversible!
🔥 Why temperature STOPS during state change?
Energy breaks intermolecular forces instead of heating. This hidden energy = Latent Heat. Temp only rises again after bonds break!
⬆️ Speed Up State Changes
• Increase temperature
• Increase surface area
• Increase airflow
• Reduce pressure (for boiling)
• Stirring the liquid
🧪 Compress Gas but NOT Liquid?
Gas = huge gaps → easy to squash.
Liquid = particles already touching → nearly impossible.

Smaller container → higher gas pressure (Boyle's Law)

⚡ Plasma Lab

The 4th state of matter — the most energetic in the universe!

🌌 Aurora (Northern Lights)
Charged particles from the Sun collide with gas atoms in Earth's atmosphere. Each collision strips electrons from atoms, creating ionised plasma that glows in brilliant curtains of colour.
🌡️ Temperature: ~1,000–5,000°C
